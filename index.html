01

Overview & Goals

GT:NH Processing+ is an addon for GregTech: New Horizons that adds new processing chains based in materials chemistry. The goal is not to make the game harder for its own sake, it's to fill gaps in the progression and make the modpack feel a little more complete.

GTNH already has a bunch of plastics: polyethylene, PTFE, PPS, PBI, and others, they each have a clear use. What's missing is the middle ground, the materials between stuff like Kevlar and PE. This addon fills that space with seven new chains that each have a new identity, a different game function, and a reason to exist that isn't "more steps for the sake of it." or "hey im used for one thing".

Core Principle

Every step in every chain either produces something useful or teaches the player something about the material. No filler. If a step can't justify its existence, it doesn't exist.

What this addon is

A content expansion that deepens existing chemistry rather than replacing it. New chains use existing GTNH materials as inputs. They produce new outputs. They integrate into existing infrastructure. They respect the difficulty curve already established by the pack.

What this addon is not

A difficulty overhaul. A recipe replacer for its own sake. A mod that makes you do 40 steps to get polyethylene. Processing+ adds things alongside what exists, not on top of it in a way that buries the player (other than the specific things I do change).

02

Design Philosophy

These eight things helped me with every design decision in the addon. When a chain or mechanic is being designed, it should satisfy as many of these as possible. A chain that satisfies none of them doesn't belong.

01
Branching Routes

No single optimal path through a chain. Every branch has a different cost. The "best" choice changes as your base evolves.

02
Byproducts Matter

No output is throwaway. Every byproduct from every chain should have a use somewhere, in this mod or in base GTNH.

03
Infrastructure Pressure

Chains are designed to be undersized when first built. The game rewards players who expand, not players who build the minimum and stop.

04
Layered Depth

New tiers add layers to existing chains, they don't replace them. A player's HV infrastructure is still running and relevant at IV.

05
Named Lines

Materials critical to tier progression graduate to dedicated and constant lines with internal loops, sorta like the platline.

06
Diverse Challenges

Not every chain is a throughput problem. Some test chemical layout, some test automation logic, some test resource routing. Variety keeps it interesting.

07
Web of Dependencies

No chain stands alone. Every chain touches at least two other products, either as a consumer or a producer. Everything feeds everything.

08
Sorta Real Chemistry

Every reaction and synthesis route is based in how the material is actually made industrially. The game heavily simplifies it, but the logic is there.

On Difficulty

The difficulty in these chains should come from what they demand of your infrastructure, not from raw step count. A three-step chain that requires a new multiblock and two continuous inputs is harder than a ten-step chain that runs in copies of a single machine.

03

Processing Chains

Seven chains across the HV–LuV range. Each has a distinct chemical identity, a distinct entry point into progression, and multiple game functions that create constant demand.

Nylon-6,6
HV → EVStructuralGate
A tough engineering plastic made from oil chain feedstocks. Stronger and more heat-resistant than polyethylene, with a key synthesis step that requires a dedicated new multiblock to complete.
  • Added as a required component in HV and EV machine hull recipes
  • Required in certain multiblock construction recipes including new multiblocks added by this mod
  • New wire coating option in the LATEX, nylon-coated cables have lower cable loss and cost compared to standard rubber
  • Alternate/changed recipes for motors, pumps, pistons and conveyor covers
Carbon Fiber Composite
EVStructuralProcess
A high-performance composite made from polymer fiber that gets carbonized at extreme temperatures and combined with resin. Two distinct heat treatment stages with different atmospheric requirements make this a genuinely involved chain.
  • Superior replacement for existing basic carbon fiber, replaces it in many existing recipes at EV+ tier
  • Continuous consumable input to certain spinny multiblocks (large turbines, maceration stacks, etc), while supplied, machines run at 120–150% speed
  • New CF composite casing block used in the physical structure of new multiblocks
Silicon Carbide
EV → IVProcessGate
An inorganic ceramic made from sand and a coal byproduct at temperatures that push the limits of existing EBF coils. Chemically inert, brutally hard, and thermally stable far beyond what any plastic can handle.
  • Required upgraded casing for a higher-tier Alloy Blast Smelter, unlocking alloy recipes that exceed standard ABS temperature limits
  • Added to EV and IV machine hull recipes as well as a few casings and singleblocks
  • Enables a new smelting multiblock: the High Temperature Reaction Furnace (HTRF) — a hard gate for recipes requiring 3000K+ heat that the EBF family cannot reach. SiC ceramic blocks form its casings
Polyimide / Kapton
EV → IVStructuralGate
A high-performance polymer film that stays stable above 400°C and insulates perfectly under high voltage — the material NASA wraps spacecraft wiring in. Made in two stages from coal tar and existing GTNH nitrogen chemistry, with a useful intermediate byproduct.
  • Hard gate for IV-tier circuit assembly — Kapton film replaces PE as the substrate material for circuit boards and Advanced SMDs at this tier
  • Required in casings for precision and electronics-adjacent multiblocks: circuit assembly lines, Precision Laser Engravers, and new cleanroom-type structure added by this mod
  • Polyamic acid byproduct used in specific recipes
  • Precursor for polyimide aerogel (planned v1.1 update)
Polylactic Acid (PLA)
HVProcessStructural
The only chain in the mod that starts with crops instead of oil. Bacteria ferment sugars into lactic acid, which gets converted into a biodegradable plastic. Completely distinct feedstock identity from everything else here.
  • Required plastic component in certain ore processing multiblocks, specifically wet-process equipment like upgraded ore washing plants and large chemical bath setups
  • Consumable PLA filtration membranes used in water purification steps — requires an actual PLA line
  • Helps producing bioethanol, making biofuel generation a lot more efficient — a free bonus for players who invest in it
White Graphite (Hexagonal Boron Nitride)
LuVProcessGate
Called "white graphene" — same layered structure as graphite, same lubricating properties, but electrically insulating instead of conductive and stable at temperatures graphite would oxidize and fall apart. Made from a new ore input reacted with ammonia at high temperature.
  • New high-tier lubricant fluid — required in LuV+ machine operations. Standard lubricant still works below LuV, but LuV machines require hBN lubricant, ensuring a dedicated setup
  • New Ceramic Reaction Vessel (CRV) multiblock — a large structure whose inner lining requires hBN ceramic blocks in quantity. The only multiblock that can safely contain certain exotic molten metal mixtures for high-tier alloy recipes
  • Replaces certain plastics in LuV and ZPM circuit recipes, completing the circuit plastic progression: PE → Kapton (IV) → hBN (LuV/ZPM)
Silica Aerogel
IV → LuVProcessGate
A material that sounds fake — a solid that's up to 99% air, with the lowest thermal conductivity of any known solid. Made by removing liquid from a gel without letting it collapse, using a high-pressure supercritical drying process that requires dedicated infrastructure.
  • Aerogel catalyst supports are consumable inputs for the new Catalytic Reaction Chamber (CRC) — a specialised reactor for advanced gas and high-temperature chemical reactions that can't run in a standard LCR without the catalytic surface area aerogel provides
  • The CRC requires Carbon Fiber composite casings to build, requiring another line to be operational first
  • Recipes tagged "requires catalytic environment" only run inside a CRC, similar to how semiconductor recipes require a cleanroom
  • Aerogel insulation panels used in certain high-tier multiblocks
04

Uses

No chain has one use. The stuff below are either like this: one chain's output feeds another's input, or building one chain enables or accelerates another. This is intentional — it mirrors the design philosophy of GTNH's existing lines. (All lines below may have specific use cases not shown here)

Chain Dependencies & Outputs
Kapton
──→
Aerogel (future): polyimide aerogel variant uses Kapton line as precursor. PAA byproduct feeds specific coating-like recipes.
PLA
──→
Biofuel infrastructure: fermentation setup built for PLA improves bioethanol and biofuel as a sort of side effect.
Carbon Fiber
──→
CRC construction: CF composite casings are required to build the Catalytic Reaction Chamber. Also consumed continuously by certain multiblock hatches.
Silicon Carbide
──→
HTRF: gates the High Temperature Reaction Furnace. HTRF then requires hBN lubricant to run at full efficiency.
Hexagonal Boron Nitride
──→
CRV construction (consumes large quantity of hBN blocks) + continuous hBN lubricant demand + LuV/ZPM circuit substrates also requiring Kapton.
Aerogel
──→
CRC operation: consumed continuously as catalyst supports. CRC unlocks advanced chemistry that benefits every other chain's efficiency.
Nylon-6,6
──→
Machine hulls and multiblock construction across HV–EV. LATEX cable coating option. Machine part recipes.
Circuit Progression Arc

This addon completes a circuit substrate ladder across all tiers: Polyethylene (MV/HV/EV) -> Kapton film (IV) -> Hexagonal Boron Nitride (LuV/ZPM). Each material is justified for its tier, PE is cheap and functional, Kapton handles extreme heat, hBN handles extreme heat AND manages electrical/thermal simultaneously.

06

Future Updates

v1.0 ships with the seven chains above. Future updates expand the aerogel line and add additional chains where needed / when interesting.

v1.1 — Aerogel Variants

The silica aerogel chain in v1.0 is designed as the base case for a tiered aerogel progression. Two variants are planned:

Carbon Aerogel

Used in supercapacitor and energy storage components. Distinct from silica aerogel, distinct electrical identity.

Polyimide Aerogel

Uses the Kapton line's PAA as the precursor, then supercritical dried the same way as silica aerogel. Higher tier coils for extreme multiblocks. Directly connects two v1.0 chains.

v1.2 — Candidate Chains

To be scoped based on community feedback and progression balance after v1.0 release. Early candidates include additional engineered ceramics and bio-based chemical chains that extend the PLA infrastructure. Oh also Luv+ content aswell.

Out of Scope

This addon will not add parallel routes to materials that already have established chains in base GTNH. New chains produce genuinely new outputs. Existing GTNH materials may appear as inputs, never as competing outputs.
